Privacy Policy

Our commitment to transparency and the protection of your data.

Last updated: July 30, 2025

1. Introduction and Commitment

Welcome to the Privacy Policy of the Bruno Miranda Advocacy law office. Your privacy and the security of your personal data are of utmost importance to us. This document details how we collect, use, share, and protect your information, in full compliance with the General Data Protection Law (LGPD - Law No. 13.709/2018).

2. Collection and Use of Personal Information

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us so that we can deliver our services and communicate with you.

  • Contact Information: Name, email, and phone number, provided through our contact forms, chat, or WhatsApp, are used exclusively to respond to your requests, schedule consultations, and provide information about our services.
  • Case Information: Details and documents related to your legal case are collected for the provision of contracted legal services, protected under professional secrecy.
  • Browsing Data (Cookies): We use cookies to optimize your experience on our website. This data (such as pages visited and time spent) helps us understand how users interact with our content and improve the site's functionality. You can manage the use of cookies through your browser settings.

3. Purpose of Data Processing

The collected data is used for the following purposes:

  • To provide and manage the contracted legal services.
  • To communicate with you, answer questions, and provide support.
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations.
  • To analyze site traffic to optimize the user experience and the effectiveness of our communications.
  • To ensure the security of our systems and clients.

4. Data Sharing

We do not share your personally identifiable information with third parties, except in the following circumstances:

  • With your explicit consent.
  • For the execution of legal services that require the collaboration of third parties (such as experts or correspondents), always under a strict confidentiality agreement.
  • When required by law, court order, or to protect our legal rights.

5. Your Rights as a Data Subject (LGPD)

You, as the data subject, have rights guaranteed by the LGPD and can, at any time, request:

  • Confirmation of the existence of processing of your data.
  • Access to your data.
  • Correction of incomplete, inaccurate, or outdated data.
  • Anonymization, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary data or data processed in non-compliance with the law.
  • Portability of your data to another service provider.
  • Deletion of personal data processed with your consent.
  • Information about the entities with which we have shared your data.

To exercise your rights, please contact us through the channels provided on this site.

6. Data Security and Storage

We employ robust technical and administrative security measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access and from accidental or unlawful situations of destruction, loss, or alteration. Information is retained only for the time necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including legal and contractual obligations.

7. Changes to this Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legislation. We recommend that you review it regularly to stay informed.

8. Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, please contact us. We are available to clarify any issue.
